About Me

Research Interests

I am currently a Master's student at McGill University and the Montreal Institute of Learning Algorithms (MILA) where I study applied mathematics under the supervision of Professor Courtney Paquette . My research applies ideas from high dimensional statistics and random matrix theory to solve problems in the intersection of convex optimization, machine learning, and deep learning.

Previously I worked on developing an algorithm to assess patients risk for recurring heart attacks using survival analysis. Now I work on developing average case complexities, exact dynamics, and generalization error for high dimensional stochastic algorithms.

As part of my research internship at Valence Discovery I am applying unsupervised models to learn a disentangled representation of molecular space in order to to optimize the drug discovery process.

Hobbies

In my free time, I enjoy playing classical music on the piano, particularly Bach and Chopin. I also like to find ways to automate tasks in my life through scripting in Python. Lastly, I occassionally watch sports and play basketball.
